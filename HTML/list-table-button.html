Mythical Creatures

Dragon
A dragon is a mythical creature found in the folklore of cultures throughout the world. It is roughly serpentine in form, and often possesses features drawn from different animals, such as wings and claws.[1] In nearly all cultures, it is conceived of as a powerful and awe-inspiring creature
Phoenix
The phoenix is a mythical bird with origins traced to ancient Egypt, where it was associated with the Bennu bird, a sacred creature linked to the sun god Ra and creation cycles.
Vampire
A vampire is a mythical creature with a human-like appearance that subsists by feeding on the vital essence (generally, the blood) of humans. In European folklore, vampires are undead humanoid creatures that often visited loved ones and caused mischief or deaths in the neighbourhoods which they inhabited while they were alive.
WereWolf
Like Dislike A werewolf is a mythological creature that is typically described as a human who can transform into a wolf or a hybrid wolf-human form, often during a full moon. The term "werewolf" comes from the Old English words "wer" (man) and "wulf" (wolf)
